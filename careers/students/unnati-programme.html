UNNATI Programme

Programme Details

Background and Mission

Launched in 2019, the UNNATI (UNispace Nanosatellite Assembly & Training by ISRO) programme is India's initiative to enhance the capabilities of other nations in assembling, integrating, and testing nanosatellites. It aims to foster international collaboration and share India's expertise in space technology.

Course Structure (8 Weeks)

  • Weeks 1-2: Theoretical foundations on satellite technology, orbital mechanics, and space systems engineering.
  • Weeks 3-6: Intensive hands-on training on the assembly, integration, and testing (AIT) of a 1U CubeSat.
  • Weeks 7-8: Mission planning, launch procedures, and processing of satellite data.

Who Should Apply?

The programme is designed for engineers and scientists from academic and research institutions in developing countries. Candidates should have a postgraduate or final-year undergraduate degree in a relevant field of science or engineering.

Sponsorship and Logistics

The UNNATI programme is fully sponsored by ISRO. This includes round-trip airfare, accommodation, living expenses, and all training materials for the selected international participants.
