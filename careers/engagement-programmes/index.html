ISRO Research Fellowship Programme

ISRO offers prestigious research fellowships to bright and motivated individuals who wish to pursue doctoral research (Ph.D.) in areas of space science, technology, and applications. This programme provides a platform for researchers to work on cutting-edge projects alongside ISRO's top scientists, contributing directly to India's space endeavours.

Key Objectives:

  • To promote original research in advanced space-related domains.
  • To develop a skilled workforce and future leaders for the Indian space programme.
  • To foster collaboration between ISRO and academic institutions.
Eligibility: Master's degree (M.E./M.Tech/M.Sc.) in a relevant engineering or science discipline with an excellent academic record.
Duration: The fellowship is initially for two years, extendable based on performance review.
Focus Areas: Propulsion, Aerodynamics, Control Systems, Space Science, Remote Sensing, GIS, and more.

How to Apply:

Notifications for Research Fellowships are posted on our official careers portal. Applications are typically accepted online, and selection is based on academic merit and performance in a written test and/or interview.

Scientist/Engineer Trainee Programme

This is a flagship programme to induct young, talented graduates directly into the scientific and engineering cadre of ISRO. Trainees undergo a one-year intensive induction training course at the ISRO Training Centre, covering various aspects of space technology before being placed as Scientist/Engineer 'SC' in different ISRO centres.

Programme Highlights:

  • Structured, multi-disciplinary training covering both theoretical and practical aspects of space technology.
  • Placement in high-profile projects upon successful completion of training.
  • A direct pathway to a rewarding career at the forefront of space exploration.
Eligibility: B.E./B.Tech or equivalent in Electronics, Mechanical, Computer Science, and other specified disciplines with a first-class degree.
Selection: Based on performance in the ISRO Centralised Recruitment Board (ICRB) written examination followed by an interview.

How to Apply:

The ICRB examination is announced annually through our website and national newspapers. Candidates can apply online through the official ISRO careers portal when the notification is active.

Technical & Scientific Assistant Positions

ISRO regularly recruits individuals with diplomas in engineering and bachelor's degrees in science for the roles of Technical and Scientific Assistants. These professionals form the backbone of our technical workforce, providing crucial support in the testing, integration, quality control, and maintenance of complex systems.

Role and Responsibilities:

  • Assisting scientists and engineers in the assembly and testing of satellite and launch vehicle components.
  • Operating and maintaining sophisticated laboratory and testing equipment.
  • Data acquisition, processing, and documentation for various projects.
Eligibility: First-class Diploma in a relevant Engineering discipline or a First-class B.Sc. degree in fields like Physics, Chemistry, or Mathematics.
Selection: Based on performance in a written test and a skill test to assess practical knowledge.

How to Apply:

Vacancies for these positions are advertised by individual ISRO centres based on their specific requirements. Keep an eye on the "Current Openings" section of our careers portal for the latest advertisements.
