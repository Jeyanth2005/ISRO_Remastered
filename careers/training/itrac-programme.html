ISRO Training Centre (ITRAC)

Programme Details

Purpose and Structure

The ISRO Training Centre (ITRAC) is the primary induction hub for newly recruited Scientist/Engineers 'SC'. It conducts a rigorous one-year residential training program designed to transform bright engineering graduates into skilled space scientists ready to contribute to India's missions.

One-Year Induction Training

  • Semester 1: A common foundation course for all recruits, covering subjects like Orbital Mechanics, Propulsion, Aerodynamics, and Satellite Systems.
  • Semester 2: Specialization tracks based on the recruit's discipline and ISRO's requirements, such as Launch Vehicle Technology, Satellite Technology, or a specific application area.
  • The training methodology includes classroom lectures from senior ISRO scientists, laboratory work, seminars, and extensive visits to various ISRO facilities.

Selection and Placement

Selection is exclusively for candidates who have successfully cleared the ISRO Centralised Recruitment Board (ICRB) written exam and interview. Upon successful completion of the one-year training programme at ITRAC, the trainees are formally inducted as Scientist/Engineer 'SC' and are posted to different ISRO centres across the country based on their specialization and organizational needs.
