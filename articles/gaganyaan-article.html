Gaganyaan: India's First Human Spaceflight Mission

Gaganyaan is India's ambitious human spaceflight program aimed at demonstrating indigenous capability to undertake human space flight missions. The program will make India the fourth nation in the world to launch humans into space from its own soil.

Mission Objectives

  • Launch humans into orbit at 400km altitude for 3 days
  • Safely return crew to Earth, landing in Indian waters
  • Demonstrate human spaceflight capabilities
  • Develop essential technologies for human space missions

Key Components

Component Description
Crew Module Habitable space capsule carrying three astronauts
Service Module Contains propulsion, power, and life support systems
Launch Vehicle Human-rated LVM3 (GSLV Mk III) rocket
Crew Escape System Emergency escape mechanism for crew safety

Mission Preparation

  • Extensive ground testing of all systems
  • Two uncrewed missions before human flight
  • Astronaut training in specialized facilities
  • Development of space suits and life support systems
  • Creation of new launch pad and mission control facilities
