Upcoming Launch: RISAT-2A to Enhance India's All-Weather Surveillance

Scheduled Launch: Early 2026

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is preparing for the launch of RISAT-2A, an advanced radar imaging satellite, in early 2026. The satellite will be launched aboard the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota.

RISAT-2A is equipped with a state-of-the-art Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) instrument. SAR technology is unique in its ability to penetrate cloud cover and operate during both day and night, providing reliable and high-resolution imagery regardless of weather conditions. This capability is vital for a wide range of applications.

Mission Objectives:

  • Disaster Management: To provide crucial imagery for monitoring floods, cyclones, and landslides, enabling faster and more effective response.
  • Agriculture and Forestry: To monitor crop health, assess forest cover, and track changes in land use.
  • National Security: To enhance the nation's surveillance capabilities for security and strategic purposes.

The RISAT series of satellites has become a cornerstone of India's Earth observation programme. The addition of RISAT-2A to the constellation will ensure the continuity and enhancement of these critical all-weather observation services for the nation.
