Witness History: Public Viewing Gallery to Open for Gaganyaan G1 Mission Launch

Event Date: December 2025 (Exact Date TBD)

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is thrilled to invite the public to witness a historic milestone in India's space journey. The Launch View Gallery at the Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota, will be open for the public to watch the launch of the first uncrewed test flight of the Gaganyaan mission (G1).

This is a unique opportunity for space enthusiasts to experience the thunderous roar and majestic sight of the human-rated LVM3 rocket as it lifts off, carrying the Gaganyaan orbital module on its first journey to space. The G1 mission is a critical test of the launch vehicle, the crew module's systems, and the re-entry and recovery operations, paving the way for India's first human spaceflight.

"We want the people of India to be a part of this journey. Opening the viewing gallery for this crucial Gaganyaan test flight is our way of sharing this moment of national pride with the entire country. We hope it inspires our youth to dream big." - Director, Satish Dhawan Space Centre

The exact launch date in December 2025 is subject to final mission readiness and weather conditions. Registration for a limited number of seats in the viewing gallery will be opened on the ISRO website on a first-come, first-served basis. Please stay tuned for the official announcement regarding the registration portal and dates.

About ISRO Events: ISRO is committed to public outreach and engagement. We regularly host events, workshops, and seminars to promote scientific temper and to keep the public informed about the latest developments in the Indian space program.
