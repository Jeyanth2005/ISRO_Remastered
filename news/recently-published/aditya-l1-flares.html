Aditya-L1 Captures Stunning High-Resolution Images of Solar Flares

Bengaluru | August 29, 2025

India's first solar observatory, Aditya-L1, has successfully captured high-resolution images of recent solar flares, providing scientists with valuable data to study the Sun's dynamic activities. The spacecraft, positioned at the Sun-Earth Lagrange Point 1 (L1), has been continuously monitoring our star since it became operational.

The images, captured by the Solar Ultraviolet Imaging Telescope (SUIT) and the Visible Emission Line Coronagraph (VELC) payloads, reveal the intricate structures of the solar atmosphere during these energetic eruptions. This data is crucial for understanding the physics of solar flares and their potential impact on space weather, which can affect satellites, communication networks, and power grids on Earth.

ISRO scientists are currently analyzing the data to better understand the mechanisms that trigger these massive releases of energy from the Sun. The Aditya-L1 mission represents a significant contribution by India to the global effort of monitoring and understanding solar phenomena.
