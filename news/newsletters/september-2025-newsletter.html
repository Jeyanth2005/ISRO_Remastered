Cosmic Chronicles: September 2025 Edition

Monthly Newsletter | Published October 01, 2025

Welcome to the September 2025 edition of Cosmic Chronicles, your monthly update from the Indian Space Research Organisation. This month has been marked by a significant commercial launch and groundbreaking findings from our solar observatory.

PSLV-C58 Successfully Launches Commercial Payloads

On September 15, ISRO's reliable Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV-C58) successfully launched 36 satellites for the UK-based company OneWeb. This launch from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre further solidifies ISRO's position as a leading player in the global commercial satellite launch market. The mission demonstrated the LVM3's capability to handle heavy payloads and complex deployment sequences.

Aditya-L1 Delivers New Solar Insights

India's first solar observatory, Aditya-L1, continues to provide invaluable data. This month, analysis of high-resolution images of solar flares has revealed new information about the energy distribution within these phenomena. Our scientists are working to correlate this data with space weather events, aiming to improve our prediction models for solar storms that could impact Earth.

Student Outreach: Fostering the Next Generation

This month, ISRO centres across the country hosted over 2,000 students as part of our ongoing outreach initiatives. Students participated in workshops on rocketry, satellite design, and had the opportunity to interact with ISRO scientists and engineers. We remain committed to inspiring the youth to pursue careers in STEM and contribute to India's space-faring future.
