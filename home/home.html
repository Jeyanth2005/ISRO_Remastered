About ISRO

ISRO: Pioneering India's Journey into Space

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is India's national space agency, headquartered in Bengaluru. Established on August 15, 1969, by the visionary physicist Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, ISRO is a testament to India's self-reliance and prowess in space technology. The agency operates under the Department of Space (DOS), which is directly overseen by the Prime Minister of India.

Major Achievements

Chandrayaan-3

India's historic lunar landing mission that successfully achieved a soft landing on the Moon's south pole region, making India the fourth country to land on the Moon.

Mangalyaan (MOM)

India's first interplanetary mission that successfully reached Mars orbit on its first attempt, demonstrating ISRO's capability in deep space missions.

LVM3 (GSLV Mk III)

India's most powerful rocket, capable of launching 4-ton class satellites to Geosynchronous Transfer Orbit (GTO).

AstroSat

India's first dedicated multi-wavelength space observatory, studying celestial sources in X-ray, optical and UV spectral bands simultaneously.

Gaganyaan

India's first human spaceflight program, aiming to demonstrate human spaceflight capability by launching a crew of 3 to an orbit of 400 km.

NISAR

A joint Earth-observing mission between NASA and ISRO, designed to measure Earth's changing ecosystems and dynamic surfaces.
